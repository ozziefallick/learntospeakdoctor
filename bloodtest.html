Healthy Behaviors Resource Center

Exercise

Why: The American Heart Association recommends 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity each week to prevent heart disease. You can walk 30 minutes each day during your lunch break and reach this goal. Learn more at [heart.org/activityrecommendations].

How: Find a walking path and keep a log of your activity at [www.startwalkingnow.org].

Why: The American Heart Association recommends moderate muscle-strengthening twice a week. You can lift weights during the commercials of your two favorite programs and reach this goal. Learn more at [heart.org/activityrecommendations] and [www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/77017.php].

How: Start with a basic, equipment-free workout at [www.sparkpeople.com].

Why: Biking to work not only count as moderate aerobic activity, but it also saves on transit, parking, and gas expenses. A recent study by the British Medical Association shows that biking 20 miles a week can reduce heart disease risk by 50% over a lifetime. Read more at [www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/225940.php].

How: Plan your route and check your safety gear with a beginner's biking guide at [www.biketoworkinfo.org/resources/rsc_guide-beginner.cfm].

Your Blood Test Results

Cholesterol

What This Means

It is ideal to have cholesterol in the lower (green) range.

It is beneficial to have low HDL ("good" cholesterol) level.

High LDL ("bad" cholesterol) levels can be a potential health hazard.

Suggestions

You can reduce your LDL by:
substituting olive oil for butter
taking a walk during lunch
eat fish 2x per week

You can raise your HDL by:
quitting smoking
eating more fiber

Complete Blood Count

What This Means

Red blood cells help deliver oxygen to the body. It is ideal to have a count within the green range.

White blood cells protect the body against infection. It is ideal to have a count within the green range.

Suggestions

You can raise your RBC count by:
eat leafy greens 3x per week
taking a walk during lunch

You can raise your platelet count by:
taking vitamin supplements
